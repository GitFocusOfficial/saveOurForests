What’s the Issue? Deforestation

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of trees which leads to the destruction of forests. This issue affects ecosystems and humans by contributing to climate change, biodiversity loss, and soil degradation.

Causes of Deforestation

  • Urban Construction: Forests are cleared for the expansion of urban areas, including roads, bridges, and power plants, resulting in significant loss of forest life. (PhilCHM)
  • Agriculture: Conversion of forest into agricultural land, including slash-and-burn farming (kaingin) commonly practiced in the Philippines. (PhilCHM)
  • Use for Fuel: Trees are cut down to be used as firewood or turned into charcoal for cooking and heating, especially in rural areas. (PhilCHM)
  • Commercial Purposes: Forests are cleared for oil and mining exploitation, often exacerbated by human-induced fires. (PhilCHM)
  • Illegal Logging: Both legal and illegal logging activities contribute to deforestation, depriving wildlife of their habitat. (PhilCHM)

Effects of Deforestation

  • Erosion: Loss of nutrients from soil leads to dry, infertile land and further environmental degradation. (PhilCHM)
  • Disruption of Water Cycle: Trees help regulate water absorption and release. Deforestation leads to imbalances in the ecosystem’s water cycle. (PhilCHM)
  • Flooding and Drought: Without trees to absorb excess water, periods of flood and drought become more frequent, increasing risks for nearby communities. (PhilCHM)
  • Climate Change: Trees store carbon dioxide. When forests are destroyed, the stored carbon is released back into the atmosphere, exacerbating climate change. (PhilCHM)

Definition of Deforestation

  • The clearing or thinning of forest by humans. (Britannica)
  • The removal and destruction of forests or stands of trees from land, later converted to non-forest use. (Wikipedia)

Current Status of Deforestation in the Philippines

In the Philippines, from 2001 to 2022, a total of 1.42 million hectares of tree cover was lost, representing a 7.6% decrease of the country's total tree cover of approximately 18.684 million hectares. (Climate Change Commission)

From 2002 to 2023, the Philippines lost 190,000 hectares (190 kha) of humid primary forest, which accounts for 13% of its total tree cover loss. During the same period, the total area of humid primary forest decreased by 4.1%. (Global Forest Watch)

What You Can Do as an Existing Solution

Deforestation is a critical issue, but everyone has the power to make a difference. Here’s how you can contribute effectively and sustainably:

1. Support Reforestation Projects

  • Join local reforestation initiatives like the National Greening Program or support global efforts to plant trees.
  • Volunteer your time for tree-planting activities or donate to organizations that restore degraded forest lands.
  • Example: Haribon Foundation’s Rainforestation Organizations and Advocates program works to plant native species and educate communities on forest care (Haribon Foundation, 2022).

2. Reduce Paper and Wood Consumption

  • Opt for digital alternatives to minimize paper use, such as e-books or online receipts.
  • Buy sustainable products certified by groups like the Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) to ensure ethical sourcing of wood.
  • Reuse and recycle paper and wood materials whenever possible to reduce demand for logging (Climate Change Commission, 2023).

3. Support Policies That Protect Forests

  • Advocate for stronger forest protection laws and sustainable land-use policies.
  • Stay informed about and support legislation like the Expanded National Integrated Protected Areas System (E-NIPAS) Act.
  • Push for stricter enforcement of the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) system to hold industries accountable (Philippine Clearing House Mechanism, n.d.).

4. Donate to or Volunteer with Organizations Fighting Deforestation

  • Contribute to groups like the Haribon Foundation, WWF-Philippines, or Philippine Tropical Forest Conservation Foundation.
  • Offer your skills or resources to assist their advocacy campaigns, research, or education programs.
  • Example: Haribon’s tree-adoption campaigns encourage individuals and corporations to fund reforestation (Haribon Foundation, 2022).

5. Practice Responsible Consumerism

  • Avoid products that contribute to deforestation, such as unsustainable palm oil or beef.
  • Research brands’ environmental policies to ensure your purchases align with sustainability goals (Global Forest Watch, n.d.).

6. Educate and Advocate for Change

  • Share information about deforestation’s impacts and solutions on social media to raise awareness.
  • Organize or participate in campaigns, webinars, or school programs to educate others about forest conservation (Britannica, n.d.).

7. Monitor Forest Loss Using Technology

  • Tools like Global Forest Watch allow individuals to track deforestation in real time. Stay informed and share data with communities and policymakers to prevent illegal logging (Global Forest Watch, n.d.).
By taking these steps, you can help protect forests, promote sustainability, and inspire others to join the fight against deforestation. Together, we can make a lasting impact.
